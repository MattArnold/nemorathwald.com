Of course they don't get a say in our politics, but I am charmed by some of the celebrations being held overseas:
The Irish village of Moneygall was also trying to claim Obama as a favorite son — based on research that concluded the candidate's great-great-great grandfather, Joseph Kearney, lived there before emigrating to the United States. At Moneygall's Hayes Bar, an American flag fluttered outside window Tuesday and local band Hardy Drew and the Nancy Boys played their rousing folk song "There's No One as Irish as Barack Obama." In the sleepy Japanese coastal town of Obama — which translates as "little beach" — images of him adorned banners along a main shopping street.
