That must have been amazing! me: in the VR rig, I realized that medium would fuse all my most passionate childhood interests-- animation, puppetry, games, theme park rides, computers-- into a single art form. I approached a staff member and told him I wanted to create environments. He told me there was no demand for anyone to create works in an art form that is still developing its tools, and that the only contribution I could make was with math and engineering to invent the tools. infowidget: bleah. way to kill a dream there, buddy. You know what? Widget's right. I did have a dream, and the letdown was not entirely reasonable. I was what, nineteen or so? The tools would get built in time for me to use them, and I should have figured out how to prepare myself rather than let the idea go. I bet I could eventually figure out how to get it back on track. I pretty much wrote off puppetry, animation and VR as a pipe dream, requiring a massive outlay of capital provided by a meddlesome corporation.
